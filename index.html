The Genesis of IntelGuard

IntelGuard was born out of a singular, unbreakable mission. Founded in June 2025 by our founder, Boaz Acosta, the organization was established as a direct response to the pervasive and devastating reality of child exploitation.

After witnessing the scale of abuse from predatory individuals toward the most vulnerable members of our society, Boaz made it his life's goal to turn the tide. He assembled an elite team of professionals to form the cornerstone of this non-profit.

Operations & Legal Compliance

Is IntelGuard a registered 501(c)(3) non-profit?

YES. IntelGuard is a legally registered non-profit organization under section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

IRS Official Notice Regarding TEOS Listing

Per IRS regulations and processing guidelines, newly registered non-profits do not appear instantly on the Tax Exempt Organization Search (TEOS) tool.

IRS internal processing times state that it can take anywhere from 2 weeks to 6 months (depending on seasonal traffic and backlog) for a newly recognized entity to be indexed in the public database.

IntelGuard maintains all proper federal documentation and determination letters. Furthermore, our organizational structure and non-profit protocols were established with direct advisory and oversight from an existing, verified 501(c)(3) non-profit to ensure absolute transparency and compliance.

What specific threats does IntelGuard fight against?

Our operations are focused strictly on the safety and protection of minors. We actively target and dismantle networks involving:

Grooming Prevention

How active is the IntelGuard team?

Due to the critical nature of our work and the global scope of exploitation networks, IntelGuard operates 24/7. Our intelligence team and field coordinators maintain constant vigilance to ensure that reported threats are addressed with immediate priority.
