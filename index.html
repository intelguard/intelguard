Secure Reporting Guidelines

Follow these critical steps to ensure your report is handled quickly and effectively by the cybercrime response team.

MANDATORY REPORT FORMAT

1. Threat Actor's Usernames/Aliases

List all names, handles, IDs, or accounts used by the threat actor (e.g., Discord ID, Instagram Handle, Telegram Username, email address).

Example: @dark_user, TheHackerGroup, JohnDoe@protonmail.com

2. Incident Summary (The Core Action)

Provide a concise, single-sentence summary of the incident. Do not elaborate here.

Example: Actor is threatening to release private photos unless $500 in crypto is sent.

3. Detailed Explanation & Chronology

Provide a chronological, in-depth account. Explain what happened, when it started, how they contacted you, the method of exploitation, and why you believe they targeted you or how they obtained the compromising material.

4. Secure Evidence Attachment (CRITICAL)

You MUST attach all evidence directly to the email.

  • Screenshots of chat logs and threats.
  • Video clips (if applicable).
  • Transaction IDs (if payment was attempted).
  • Ensure screenshots show dates/times and platform usernames.
